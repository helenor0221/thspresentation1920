NATURAL RESOURCES

NATURAL RESOURCES

Mexico has been ranked as the fourth most biodiverse county in the world, with over 200,000 animal and plant species. It is ranked first in terms of reptiles with 707 known species, second in mammals with 438 known species, and fourth for amphibians with 290 species.

Petroleum

The petroleum industry in Mexico makes Mexico the eleventh largest producer of oil in the world and the thirteenth largest in terms of net exports.

Silver

Mexico is the 1st producer of silver in the world. Silver pieces made in Mexico include candelabras, jars, platters, necklaces and buttons for charro outfits.

Copper

Traditionalcopper work in Mexico has its origins in the pre Hispanic period, mostly limited to Purépecha Empire (Chinese: 塔拉斯卡王國) in what are now the states of Michoacán (Chinese: 米卻肯州) and Jalisco (Chinese: 哈利斯科州).

Gold

Mexico has been a top gold-producing country for many years, and in 2018 its gold output for the year fell slightly to come in at 118.4 metric tons.

Lead

Mexico is the sixth largest lead-producing country in the world, and 40% of its production is used locally in different industrial processes that cause lead contamination of the environment.

Zinc

Mexico was the world's sixth biggest zinc miner in 2018 with the US Geological Survey estimating output of 650,000t, down from 674,000t the previous year.

Natural gas

Mexico holds 12 trillion cubic feet (Tcf) of proven gas reserves as of 2017, ranking 32nd in the world and accounting for about 0% of the world's total natural gas reserves of 6,923 Tcf. Mexico has proven reserves equivalent to 4.2 times its annual consumption.

Timber

Roundwood production totalled 44.2 million m3 in 2015. The forestry sector (including wood processing and pulp and paper) contributed USD 7.0 billion to the economy in 2011, or nearly 0.6% of the GDP.
